## PRD Template 1: Consulting Services

### Executive Summary

A professional landing page to establish credibility, showcase expertise, and convert visitors into consulting clients through clear service offerings and compelling calls-to-action.

### Problem Statement

**Target User**: Independent consultants, freelancers, small agencies

**Challenges**:
1. Difficulty establishing credibility without corporate backing
2. Unclear service differentiation in crowded market
3. No systematic way to capture and qualify leads
4. Time spent on proposals for unqualified prospects

**Success looks like**: Qualified leads booking discovery calls, clear understanding of services before first conversation.

### User Stories

#### US-1: Credibility at First Glance
**As a** potential client visiting for the first time
**I want to** quickly understand this consultant's expertise and track record
**So that** I can decide if they're worth my time to explore further

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Professional headline visible within 2 seconds
- [ ] Key credentials (years, clients, results) above the fold
- [ ] Social proof (logos, testimonials) visible without scrolling
- [ ] Clear specialization stated

#### US-2: Service Understanding
**As a** prospect evaluating options
**I want to** understand exactly what services are offered and at what investment level
**So that** I can self-qualify before reaching out

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] 3 distinct service tiers clearly explained
- [ ] Pricing or pricing model transparent
- [ ] Deliverables listed for each tier
- [ ] Clear differentiation between tiers

#### US-3: Trust Building
**As a** skeptical visitor
**I want to** see proof that this consultant delivers results
**So that** I can trust them with my business challenges

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Case studies with specific metrics
- [ ] Testimonials from named clients (or client types)
- [ ] Before/after or problem/solution format
- [ ] Relevant industry experience shown

#### US-4: Easy Next Step
**As a** interested prospect
**I want to** easily schedule a conversation
**So that** I can explore working together without friction

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Calendar booking available (Calendly or similar)
- [ ] Multiple contact options (email, form, phone optional)
- [ ] Clear expectation of what happens next
- [ ] No friction (minimal form fields)

### Functional Requirements

| Section | Required | Key Elements |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Hero | Yes | Name, specialty, value proposition, CTA |
| Authority | Yes | Stats, credentials, brief bio |
| Services | Yes | 3 tiers with pricing, features, CTAs |
| Case Studies | Yes | 2-3 projects with metrics |
| Testimonials | Recommended | 3 quotes with attribution |
| FAQ | Recommended | 4-6 common objections addressed |
| Contact | Yes | Calendar link, email, location |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Visitor → Calendar booking | >3% | Analytics + Calendly |
| Time on page | >2 minutes | Google Analytics |
| Bounce rate | <50% | Google Analytics |
| Discovery calls / month | 10+ | CRM tracking |
| Discovery → Proposal | >50% | CRM tracking |
| Proposal → Close | >30% | CRM tracking |

### Content Requirements

**Hero Section**:
- Headline: 5-10 words, outcome-focused
- Subheadline: Who you help + transformation
- Primary CTA: Action verb + benefit ("Book Your Strategy Call")

**Services Section**:
- Entry tier: Lower commitment, self-service or one-time
- Middle tier: Core offering, recurring or project-based
- Premium tier: High-touch, custom or retainer

**Case Studies**:
- Format: Challenge → Solution → Results
- Include: Industry, timeline, specific metrics
- Protect confidentiality where needed

## PRD Template 2: Portfolio (Designers/Developers)

### Executive Summary

A visual portfolio showcasing creative work, technical skills, and professional experience to attract freelance clients or full-time employment opportunities.

### Problem Statement

**Target User**: Designers, developers, creative professionals

**Challenges**:
1. Work speaks louder than words, but needs context
2. Balancing breadth vs. depth of work shown
3. Standing out in competitive creative market
4. Keeping portfolio current with best work

**Success looks like**: Inbound inquiries from ideal clients/employers, work samples that sell themselves.

### User Stories

#### US-1: Quick Work Assessment
**As a** hiring manager or potential client
**I want to** see the quality of work within 10 seconds
**So that** I can decide whether to explore further

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Best work visible immediately (hero or featured section)
- [ ] Visual thumbnails load quickly
- [ ] Professional aesthetic consistent with work quality
- [ ] Clear indication of role/specialty

#### US-2: Project Deep Dive
**As an** interested viewer
**I want to** understand the context, process, and impact of each project
**So that** I can evaluate problem-solving ability, not just final output

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Each project has dedicated detail view
- [ ] Problem/challenge clearly stated
- [ ] Process/approach explained
- [ ] Results/impact quantified where possible
- [ ] Role in project clarified (solo vs. team)

#### US-3: Skills Verification
**As a** technical evaluator
**I want to** understand the technical skills and tools used
**So that** I can assess fit for my project/role requirements

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Skills/technologies listed clearly
- [ ] Proficiency levels indicated (optional)
- [ ] Skills tied to specific projects
- [ ] Current/learning skills distinguished from mastered

#### US-4: Easy Outreach
**As someone** ready to reach out
**I want to** contact quickly with my opportunity
**So that** I can start a conversation without barriers

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Contact form or email prominent
- [ ] Availability status indicated
- [ ] Response time expectation set
- [ ] Social/professional links available

### Functional Requirements

| Section | Required | Key Elements |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Hero | Yes | Name, specialty, featured work preview |
| Portfolio | Yes | 4-8 projects with thumbnails, filters optional |
| Project Detail | Yes | Problem, process, solution, results |
| Skills | Recommended | Categorized by type (design, dev, tools) |
| About | Recommended | Background, approach, personality |
| Experience | Optional | Timeline of roles (if seeking employment) |
| Contact | Yes | Form, email, availability status |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Portfolio views / month | 500+ | Analytics |
| Project detail click-through | >30% | Event tracking |
| Contact form submissions | >2% of visitors | Form analytics |
| Response to outreach | Track inquiries | Email/CRM |
| Interview/meeting rate | >50% of inquiries | Manual tracking |

### Content Requirements

**Project Showcase**:
- 4-8 best projects (quality over quantity)
- Consistent presentation format
- Mix of project types if versatile
- Most impressive/recent first

**Project Details**:
- Challenge: What problem needed solving?
- Approach: How did you tackle it?
- Solution: What did you create?
- Impact: What results did it achieve?
- Role: What specifically did you do?

**Skills Section**:
- Group by category (Design, Development, Tools)
- Indicate proficiency if helpful
- Link skills to project examples
- Keep current (remove outdated)

## PRD Template 3: Digital Resume

### Executive Summary

A modern digital resume that presents professional experience, skills, and achievements in a format optimized for both human readers and applicant tracking systems (ATS).

### Problem Statement

**Target User**: Job seekers, career changers, professionals

**Challenges**:
1. PDF resumes are static and hard to update
2. LinkedIn profiles lack customization and control
3. Standing out among hundreds of applicants
4. Balancing completeness with scannability

**Success looks like**: Recruiters reaching out, interview requests, offers from target companies.

### User Stories

#### US-1: Quick Qualification Check
**As a** recruiter screening candidates
**I want to** assess basic qualifications in 30 seconds
**So that** I can decide whether to read deeper

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Current role and company immediately visible
- [ ] Years of experience clear
- [ ] Key skills/technologies listed
- [ ] Target role/level apparent
- [ ] Location and remote preference stated

#### US-2: Achievement Assessment
**As a** hiring manager
**I want to** understand specific accomplishments, not just responsibilities
**So that** I can evaluate potential impact at my company

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Achievements quantified with metrics
- [ ] Impact clearly stated (revenue, efficiency, scale)
- [ ] Relevant accomplishments for target role highlighted
- [ ] Career progression visible

#### US-3: Skills Verification
**As a** technical interviewer
**I want to** understand technical depth and breadth
**So that** I can prepare relevant interview questions

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Technical skills categorized clearly
- [ ] Proficiency levels indicated
- [ ] Skills connected to experience
- [ ] Projects demonstrating skills linked

#### US-4: Easy Download/Share
**As a** recruiter or HR
**I want to** download a PDF version or share a link
**So that** I can add to ATS or share with hiring team

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] PDF download available
- [ ] Clean print version
- [ ] Shareable URL
- [ ] Contact info accessible

### Functional Requirements

| Section | Required | Key Elements |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Hero | Yes | Name, target role, location, headline |
| Summary | Yes | 2-3 sentence professional summary |
| Experience | Yes | Reverse chronological, achievements focused |
| Skills | Yes | Categorized, relevant to target role |
| Education | Yes | Degrees, certifications, relevant training |
| Projects | Optional | Side projects, open source, publications |
| Contact | Yes | Email, LinkedIn, GitHub, phone optional |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Page views / month | 200+ | Analytics |
| PDF downloads | >10% of visitors | Download tracking |
| Recruiter outreach | 5+ / month | Email tracking |
| Interview requests | Track applications | Manual tracking |
| Response rate to applications | >10% | Application tracking |

### Content Requirements

**Professional Summary**:
- 2-3 sentences maximum
- Include: specialty, years of experience, key achievement
- Target role/level mentioned
- Value proposition clear

**Experience Section**:
- Reverse chronological order
- Company, title, dates, location
- 3-5 bullet points per role
- Start bullets with action verbs
- Include metrics wherever possible

**Skills Matrix**:
- Languages/Technologies
- Frameworks/Tools
- Soft skills (selectively)
- Certifications/credentials

## PRD Template 4: Enterprise Initiative Communication

### Executive Summary

An internal communication site to align employees and stakeholders around a strategic initiative, driving understanding, buy-in, and adoption through clear vision, benefits, and progress visibility.

### Problem Statement

**Target User**: Program managers, change management leads, executive sponsors

**Challenges**:
1. Initiative fatigue - employees overwhelmed by constant change
2. Unclear "why" - benefits not connected to daily work
3. Resistance to change - fear of job impact, new skills
4. Communication gaps - inconsistent messaging across org
5. Progress invisible - no way to see momentum or wins

**Success looks like**: High awareness, reduced resistance, active participation, on-track adoption metrics.

### User Stories

#### US-1: Vision Understanding
**As an** employee hearing about this initiative
**I want to** understand why this matters and what it means for me
**So that** I can decide how to engage with the change

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Business context explained in plain language
- [ ] Personal impact addressed (what changes, what doesn't)
- [ ] Benefits to my daily work clear
- [ ] Timeline and phases visible
- [ ] Executive commitment demonstrated

#### US-2: Concern Resolution
**As an** employee worried about the initiative
**I want to** get honest answers to my concerns
**So that** I can reduce anxiety and focus on my work

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] FAQ addresses real concerns (job security, workload, training)
- [ ] Feedback channels clearly available
- [ ] Leadership accessible for questions
- [ ] Updates address emerging concerns

#### US-3: Progress Visibility
**As a** stakeholder invested in this initiative
**I want to** see tangible progress and wins
**So that** I maintain confidence in the program's success

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Phase status clearly indicated
- [ ] Metrics showing movement toward goals
- [ ] Recent wins and milestones highlighted
- [ ] Blockers/challenges acknowledged honestly

#### US-4: Participation Path
**As an** employee who wants to contribute
**I want to** understand how I can get involved
**So that** I can shape the change rather than have it happen to me

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Multiple participation options (champion, working group, pilot)
- [ ] Time commitment clear for each option
- [ ] Sign-up process simple
- [ ] Recognition for contributors visible

#### US-5: Manager Enablement
**As a** people manager
**I want to** understand what my team needs to know and do
**So that** I can support them through the change

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- [ ] Manager-specific guidance available
- [ ] Team communication templates provided
- [ ] FAQ for team questions addressed
- [ ] Escalation path for concerns clear

### Functional Requirements

| Section | Required | Key Elements |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Hero | Yes | Initiative name, vision statement, sponsor, status |
| Vision & Context | Yes | Challenge, vision, why now, strategic alignment |
| Benefits | Yes | Value for employees, customers, business |
| Roadmap | Yes | Phases with status, deliverables, success metrics |
| FAQ | Yes | Address concerns honestly, feedback channels |
| Get Involved | Yes | Participation options, communication channels, contacts |
| Metrics | Recommended | Current vs. target KPIs, progress indicators |
| Leadership | Optional | Sponsor, steering committee, working groups |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Page views | 80% of employees | Analytics |
| Time on page | >3 minutes | Analytics |
| FAQ engagement | >50% click-through | Event tracking |
| Feedback submissions | Track volume | Form analytics |
| Champion sign-ups | 5% of employees | Sign-up tracking |
| Pilot enrollment | Meet target | Enrollment data |
| Awareness survey | >80% aware of initiative | Survey |
| Sentiment survey | >60% positive/neutral | Survey |
| Adoption metrics | Per initiative | Business systems |

### Content Requirements

**Vision & Context**:
- The Challenge: What problem are we solving? (data-driven)
- Our Vision: What does success look like? (inspiring)
- Why Now: Why is this urgent/important? (compelling)
- Strategic Alignment: How does this support company goals? (credibility)

**Benefits Section**:
- For Employees: Direct daily work improvements (most important)
- For Customers: Service/experience improvements
- For the Business: Financial and strategic impact
- Each benefit specific and measurable

**Roadmap**:
- 3-4 phases maximum
- Clear deliverables per phase
- Status indicators (Active/Upcoming/Complete)
- Success metrics per phase
- Realistic timelines

**FAQ**:
- Address the unspoken concerns
- Be honest about challenges
- Provide concrete answers, not corporate speak
- Include feedback channels

**Get Involved**:
- Multiple commitment levels (low/medium/high)
- Clear time expectations
- Simple sign-up process
- Recognition/incentives where appropriate

### Governance Requirements

**Update Cadence**:
| Content Type | Frequency | Owner |
|--------------|-----------|-------|
| Roadmap status | Monthly | Program lead |
| Metrics dashboard | Weekly/Monthly | Analytics |
| FAQ updates | As needed | Change management |
| News/announcements | Weekly | Communications |
| Phase transitions | At milestones | Program lead |

**Approval Process**:
- Content updates: Program lead approval
- Major messaging changes: Sponsor approval
- Metrics/status: Data verification required
- FAQ additions: Change management review

## Choosing the Right Template

### Decision Matrix

| If your goal is... | Use Template |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Generate consulting/freelance leads | Consulting Services |
| Showcase creative/technical work | Portfolio |
| Attract job opportunities | Digital Resume |
| Communicate internal initiative | Enterprise Initiative |

### Hybrid Approaches

Some situations may combine elements:

**Consultant seeking both clients AND speaking gigs**:
- Start with Consulting Services template
- Add speaking topics and past events section
- Include testimonials from event organizers

**Developer seeking both freelance AND full-time**:
- Start with Portfolio template
- Add detailed experience section from Resume
- Indicate openness to both opportunity types

**Initiative site for BOTH internal AND external audiences**:
- Create two versions (internal detailed, external summary)
- Or use audience-specific sections
- Be careful about confidential information

```

```


### Digital Resume
```
"Create a PRD for my digital resume targeting [role type] positions at [company type/size]. I have [X] years of experience in [area] and my key achievement is [accomplishment]."
```

### Enterprise Initiative
```
"Create a PRD for an internal communication site for [initiative name], a [type of program] sponsored by [executive]. The main challenge is [problem] and success means [adoption target] by [timeline]. Key concerns to address are [concerns]."
```
